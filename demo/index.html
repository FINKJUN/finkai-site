Financial Intelligence for Multi-Entity Groups

The CFO doesn't need more data. She needs the right answer, traced to a voucher.

FINK is an AI-powered financial intelligence layer for CFOs and Controllers running multi-entity businesses on Tally and QuickBooks. It answers natural-language questions about the books, surfaces variances with explanations, and produces statutory-grade reports — without anyone touching Excel.

01
Reduced hallucination risk
AI translates English into structured queries. The database returns the numbers. Answers are computed arithmetically — the model never invents a figure.
02
Voucher-traceable
Every number on every screen drills back to its source journal voucher in Tally or QBO. No black boxes.
03
Multi-entity native
Consolidation, FX translation, and inter-company elimination are not add-ons. They are how the system thinks.

Context for First-Time Viewers

What you're looking at

Sample data
All eight screens use a fictional Indian multi-entity group — Sundaram Steels — comprising three Tally-based Indian entities (Steels, Alloys, Logistics) and one QuickBooks-based UAE FZE (Trading). This lets the multi-entity, multi-currency, and inter-company elimination story show up without needing real customer data.
Interactivity
These are working HTML, not Figma exports. Click rows, toggle tabs, hover KPIs, navigate between screens — the interactions are part of the spec. Static screenshots lose half the meaning.
Design language
Editorial finance-terminal aesthetic — Bloomberg meets premium financial stationery. Serif for display, monospace for numbers, warm off-white background, oxblood and deep-green for variance directions. The opposite of generic SaaS dashboard blue.
Built faithfully to the product
Tool names, hierarchy levels (L1–L6), role names, statement structure, and EBITDA bridge logic mirror the actual FINKAI codebase. No screen invents a feature that doesn't exist in the real product spec.
